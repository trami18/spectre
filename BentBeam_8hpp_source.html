66 /*!
67  * \brief A state of pure bending of an elastic beam in 2D
68  *
69  * \details This solution describes a 2D slice through an elastic beam of length
70  * \f$L\f$ and height \f$H\f$, centered around (0, 0), that is subject to a
71  * bending moment \f$M=\int T^{xx}y\mathrm{d}y\f$ (see e.g.
72  * \cite ThorneBlandford2017, Eq. 11.41c for a bending moment in 1D). The beam
73  * material is characterized by an isotropic and homogeneous constitutive
74  * relation \f$Y^{ijkl}\f$ in the plane-stress approximation (see
75  * `Elasticity::ConstitutiveRelations::IsotropicHomogeneous`). In this scenario,
76  * no body-forces \f$f_\mathrm{ext}^j\f$ act on the material, so the
77  * \ref Elasticity equations reduce to \f$\nabla_i T^{ij}=0\f$, but the bending
78  * moment \f$M\f$ generates the stress
79  *
80  * \f{align}
81  * T^{xx} &= \frac{12 M}{H^3} y \\
82  * T^{xy} &= 0 = T^{yy} \text{.}
83  * \f}
84  *
85  * By fixing the rigid-body motions to
86  *
87  * \f[
88  * \xi^x(0,y)=0 \quad \text{and} \quad \xi^y\left(\pm \frac{L}{2},0\right)=0
89  * \f]
90  *
91  * we find that this stress is produced by the displacement field
92  *
93  * \f{align}
94  * \xi^x&=-\frac{12 M}{EH^3}xy \\
95  * \xi^y&=\frac{6 M}{EH^3}\left(x^2+\nu y^2-\frac{L^2}{4}\right)
96  * \f}
97  *
98  * in terms of the Young's modulus \f$E\f$ and the Poisson ration \f$\nu\f$ of
99  * the material. The corresponding strain \f$S_{ij}=\partial_{(i}\xi_{j)}\f$ is
100  *
101  * \f{align}
102  * S_{xx} &= -\frac{12 M}{EH^3} y \\
103  * S_{yy} &= \frac{12 M}{EH^3} \nu y \\
104  * S_{xy} &= S_{yx} = 0
105  * \f}
106  *
107  * and the potential energy stored in the entire infinitesimal slice is
108  *
109  * \f[
110  * \int_{-L/2}^{L/2} \int_{-H/2}^{H/2} U dy\,dx = \frac{6M^2}{EH^3}L \text{.}
111  * \f]
112  */

143  "A 2D slice through an elastic beam which is subject to a bending "

177  /// Return potential energy integrated over the whole beam material
178  double potential_energy() const {
179  return 6. * length_ * square(bending_moment_) /
